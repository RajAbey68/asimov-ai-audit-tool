Transfer Attack Testing

Adversarial Attack Mitigation Techniques Risk: High Sector: Financial Services

Life-Wise Insight

Research from Stanford in 2023 found that Transfer Attack Testing detected 71% of potential vulnerabilities missed by traditional security approaches. One retail organization estimated savings of $3.2M after identifying transfer vulnerabilities in their recommendation system that could have been exploited for market manipulation. Both NIST and EU AI regulatory frameworks now specifically reference transfer attack resilience as a required security control for high-risk systems.

Description: Develop and implement Transfer Attack Testing controls to detect and mitigate adversarial examples transferable from one model to another.

Explainability: Transfer attacks occur when adversarial examples created for one model can successfully fool different models. This control requires testing for cross-model vulnerability to ensure that defenses protect against transferable adversarial examples.

Expected Evidence: Documentation of transfer attack testing procedures, results of transfer vulnerability assessments, and mitigation measures implemented to address identified vulnerabilities.

Frameworks Reference:

EU AI Law: Article 15 - Accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity

NIST 800-53: SA-11 Developer Testing and Evaluation, RA-5 Vulnerability Monitoring and Scanning
